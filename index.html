Messages from a Blind Spot

A passionate plea to open eyes and hearts to Kashmir’s pain.

The Indian state has unleashed yet another spell of indiscriminate force on the people of Kashmir, who were mourning the death of a rebel fighter killed in cold bold on 8th of July, 2016. To crush the protesters, the state forces have resorted to brutal violence that has left 50 civilians dead, hundreds blinded with pellet shotguns and thousands grievously injured with live bullet wounds.

The beleaguered vale of Kashmir is reeling under a suffocating siege and a total clampdown on any form of communication with the outside world. This Cre-de-Coeur is a passionate attempt at turning the attention of the world community toward the blind-spot Kashmir has turned into over the years.

Kashmir in Context of Current Crisis

Jammu and Kashmir is under a brutal state aggression that aims to control the population, crackdown on collective protests, restrict all civilian rights and break the will of the people who demand the occupation to uphold its right to self-determination.

The present violence is continuation of this state repression since decades, whereby people’s human rights have been grossly violated through systematic oppressive tactics by the state apparatus.

Thus far, militarily occupied Jammu Kashmir with over 700,000 Indian armed forces personnel have been complicit of killing 70, 000 people, over 8000 enforced disappearances and countless cases of torture and sexual violence. In this occupied territory, the Indian state has given legal impunity to its armed forces as to exonerate potential war crimes. The Indian state not only stands in violation of its own laws but international laws and in particular the Geneva Conventions.

Jammu Kashmir Coalition of Civil Society (JKCCS), on behalf of the resilient people of Jammu and Kashmir launches - Messages from a Blind Spot - a compassionate campaign to remind the international community of the sufferings caused by the aggression of the State in a region where people’s rightful demand to self-determination is trampled under the jackboots with brute force.

Please go through the various sections and familiarize yourself with the crisis. Even a small contribution from you can make a big difference towards upholding human rights in Kashmir. We thank you for showing compassion and appreciate your time and concern.

Injured

During the current onslaught of state violence, the indiscriminate use of pellet shotguns by its forces has resulted in an estimated 105 civilians being partially or totally blinded. These 12-guage, pump-action shotguns first put to use during the 2010 uprising are maiming and blinding the unarmed civilians which include kids as young as 5-year-old and elderly as old as 80-year-old. 
Fearing profiling and reprisals of injured youths by police, hospital officials have assigned serial numbers to pellet gun victims to hide their identity from undercover police officers who are roaming the hospitals hunting for injured protesters.

Zuhra Majeed

Four-year-old Zuhra Majeed was hit by pellets in her legs and abdomen after her family was targeted by police outside their home on the outskirts of Srinagar’s Qamarwari on July 10.

Insha Malik

Insha Malik, a resident of south Kashmir’s Shopian district, was blinded in both eyes by pellets fired by forces on July 12. The 14-year-old was hit by nearly 100 pellets while she, according to her family, was inside their house in Sedow village.

Mir Arafat

13-year-old Mir Arafat was hit by pellets fired by government forces to disperse protesters in Khanabal area of Anantnag. Hundreds of pellets penetrated Arafat’s body, from his face to the lower abdomen.  Doctors said Arafat has pellets in his heart, abdomen and intestines, some even touched his spine.

Tamana Ashraf

Nine-year-old Tamana Ashraf of Ganderbal district is another victim being treated at the Srinagar hospital. She was sitting at the window in her house when pellets whizzed by, hitting her left eye, her mother Shamima told Al Jazeera. "I saw a small iron ball in her eye. When we tried to hospitalise her, police stopped us and beat us up. I was crying to see what they had done to my daughter. Luckily we managed to reach here," she said.

Shabir Ahmad

Tragedy befell 20-year-old Shabir Ahmad who was walking towards his home in Kashmir’s Pulwama district. Some youths were protesting on the road and suddenly he felt a spray of hot iron particles piercing his face completely damaging the tissue of his right eye.

Danish

Danish, 22, from Rainawari locality of the downtown Srinagar, has been hit in both the eyes and stares into complete darkness. His father, a labourer, sits beside him with drooping shoulders. Danish is not aware of his plight and thinks he can see once his bandage is removed. “I work as a salesman for a private company, I can’t stay here I have to get back to work” he says.
