Messages from a Blind Spot

18 days, an estimated 50 civilians dead, and thousands injured.

Since the July 8 th of 2016, the Indian state has unleashed indiscriminate force on the people of Jammu Kashmir. To crackdown on the people who were mourning the death of an armed fighter, the state has held the Kashmir region under a suffocating siege. To crush the protesters, the state has resorted to brutal violence that has left 50 civilians dead and thousands injured.

Read about it

Where it began

Among the thousands of casualties lies a horrific usage of pellet shotguns. In the name of non lethal weapons, the state’s indiscriminate use of pellet shot guns has resulted in an estimated 70 civilians being partially or totally blinded. The pellet shotguns have been first put to use in 2010, have been banned internationally by various organisations and governments. They are incapable of distinction or proportionality of force during ‘crowd control’ situations. Jammu Kashmir is reeling under a state agression that aims to control the population, crackdown on collective protests, restrict all civilian rights and break the will of the people who demand freedom and self determination.

The present violence is a continuation of state agression since decades, whereby people’s demand for right of self determination has been curbed under a brutal crackdown. Thus far, militarily occupied Jammu Kashmir with over 700,000 Indian armed forces personnel have been complicit of killing 70, 000 people, over 8000 enforced disappearances and countless cases of torture and sexual violence.

In this occupied territory, the Indian state has given legal impunity to its armed forces as to exonerate potential war crimes. The Indian state not only stands in violation of its own laws but international laws and in particular the Geneva conventions.

Today, JKCCS, on behalf of the resilient and courageous people of Jammu and Kashmir launches - Messages from a Blind Spot - a campaign to remind the international community to the suffering caused by the aggression of the State in a region where people demand their basic right to freedom.Stand for the people of Jammu and Kashmir.
